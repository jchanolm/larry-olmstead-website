Larry Olmstead

Transformational Leader with Four Decades of Excellence in Media, Non-Profit Leadership, and Organizational Development

Foundation & Early Career

Larry Olmstead grew up in New York, graduating from the prestigious Bronx High School of Science. After studying journalism and political science at George Washington University, Larry embarked on a successful media career that would span decades and earn national recognition.

Media Leadership Excellence

Larry rose through the ranks of America's most prestigious newspapers, serving as City Editor of the Detroit Free Press, Special Projects Editor at The New York Times, and Managing Editor of the Miami Herald. He became a corporate officer for news and Human Resources at Knight Ridder, the Herald's parent company.

Helped lead the Miami Herald to two Pulitzer Prizes
Participated on two other Pulitzer-winning teams
Pulitzer finalist for foreign correspondence in apartheid South Africa

Entrepreneurial Leadership

After the sale of Knight Ridder in 2006, Larry launched Leading Edge Associates, a consulting firm specializing in leadership and organizational development, executive coaching, and equity and diversity. This marked his transition from media leadership to organizational transformation expertise.

Non-Profit Transformation

Following his passion for public service, Larry joined United Way Silicon Valley in 2010, rising to Vice President for Organizational Development. In 2016, he was named President & CEO of United Way of Northern California, based in Redding.

5x
Growth in staff, programs, revenue and clients served

Under Larry's transformational leadership, the agency experienced unprecedented growth, expanding its impact more than five-fold while navigating multiple challenges including wildfire disasters and the pandemic. Larry retired from the CEO position in March 2025 but continues to advise the agency as a consultant.

Academic Leadership

Larry has taught and participated in executive development programs at Northwestern and Harvard universities, sharing his expertise with emerging leaders. He is currently completing his bachelor's degree in organizational leadership at Simpson University, demonstrating his commitment to lifelong learning.

Community & Personal Life

Larry is deeply involved in tennis, holding instructor certifications from the Professional Tennis Registry. He has served in numerous volunteer roles for the United States Tennis Association, including regional Board member and national committee member for recreational league play.

Larry lives in Redding, California with his wife, Michele Chandler. The couple have two grown children and remain actively engaged in their community.
